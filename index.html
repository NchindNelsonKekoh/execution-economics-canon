Execution Economics Canon

A doctrine of institutional execution — studying why capital, policy, and design intent so often fail to become delivered outcomes, and what disciplined execution architecture requires instead.

Core Proposition

Most development failure is not a resource failure. It is an execution architecture failure — the structural gap between decision and delivery, and between design and system.

Three Core Architectures

Execution Architecture

How decisions fail to become outcomes. The structural distance between approval and delivery, and the design discipline that closes it.

Value Architecture

How value is generated, lost, or never captured across institutional, transactional, and coordination chains.

Performance Architecture

How institutions fail to become investable, governable systems — and the mechanisms that convert intent into measurable institutional performance.

Featured Insight

The Coordination Tax

Execution rarely stalls for lack of resources. It stalls through repeated alignment cycles across fragmented institutional actors — a hidden, compounding cost that most systems never price in, and that the Canon treats as a first-order variable rather than an afterthought.

Research Programme

The Canon operates as a living institutional research system, built around four standing lines of inquiry:
  • Execution failure analysis
  • Institutional design systems
  • Development effectiveness
  • Investment-readiness frameworks
Each doctrine post and working paper extends this system rather than standing apart from it.

Institutional Engagement

The Execution Economics Canon is built for direct engagement — with governments, development finance institutions, research bodies, and implementation partners working to close the distance between policy intent and delivered outcomes.

This is not commentary from the sidelines; it is a working architecture meant to be adopted, tested, and applied.
